Voice and tone

What is voice and tone?

Attends’ voice is a reflection of who we are. We should always sound like Attends. Our tone adapts appropriately to the context and situation. The best way to decide what tone to use is to ask yourself what customers are likely feeling when they encounter your message so you can decide how to respond.

For example, when Customers make a purchase for the first time, they might be feeling excited, accomplished, and maybe a little bit unsure of what to do next. We can respond to this by congratulating them and giving them the information they need to process their order.

When customers encounter an error message, they might be feeling anxious, stressed out, and that in 90% of times results in drop from the purchase. We can respond to this by stating what went wrong and giving clear direction about how to resolve the problem. We never want to use a negative tone because we are here to help people.

Voice guidelines

Voice is the foundation for communicating about Attends products and delivering brand messages. At Attends, our voice expresses the core of our personality, while bringing comfort, Confidence, freedom, dignity and a sense of connection to our users. Through careful use of the Attends voice, we help empower users to meet their personal goals and ease their life’s frustrations.

Tone Approach

Tone conveys an attitude toward both the subject matter and the reader. All content – whether it’s introductory text on the UI, an error message, or a topic in the docs – tells part of a bigger story. Good storytellers are skilled in two main areas: what they are saying (the substantive content) and how to say it to any given audience (the style and tone in which it is presented). Be a good storyteller.

Colours

Colour Anatomy

Attends default themes are derived from the Attends US Design Language color palette. The Gray family is dominant in the default themes, making use of subtle shifts in value to help organize content into distinct zones.

The core Blue family serves as the primary action color across all Attends products and experiences. Additional colors are used sparingly and purposefully.

Gradients

These gradient variations are meant to serve as solutions for any different scenarios and applications. The core Blue and Gray serve special cases in backgrounds, buttons and call to actions.

Images in use

When choosing an image to be used on our websites you must have certain considerations in mind. What’s the message you want to pass? Who are you telling it to? How do they connect with your message? are the first questions you have to ask yourself.

After that, think of the technical needs and restrictions:

  1. Is it a banner, an article post or a card/tombstone? Or could it be all of them?
  2. Depending on the decision on point 1. you need to consider the action area, the safe area and where the call to action should go.
  3. Have in mind the multiple screen sizes that will have the image on and make sure you cover those when choosing.

Usage in banners

Whenever you need to use Images on a banner, we always advise choosing backgrounds that distinguish areas for CTA readability (see above). It is also advised to have the major focus of the image on either the right or left side of the banner in order to leave enough space for title, text and CTA button.

Although we don’t advise it, for exceptional cases a 90% opacity white or Attends blue text box can be used as foreground behind the CTA area. Also have in mind the responsiveness that has to be attended. The image should work well either in Desktop, Tablet or Mobile screens. And always remember, quality is a distinguisher between a good and a bad product so, we always advise to have these sizes and a doubled size version for each screen (@2x). You can either use file formats in .JPEG or .PNG, where the second one might have transparent areas, but bear in mind the constrains the component might have.

You can find more about banners and sliders sizes and alignments on the Carousel area of our components section.

Usage in Cards

When using images for cards/tombstones like Featured Pages, Articles and Products, file formats can be used as JPEGs or PNGs. Be aware of the Card template style guidelines, in order to build proper layouts respecting brand tones and image patterns.

The image should align with the top and sides of the card and its proportions should be 4x3.5, so whatever the size of the card or the column’s width.

For example: for a 3 column split the card would have 440px width and so the Image would be 440x390px. We also recommend exporting images with twice the regular size for retina displays with the convention naming of i.e. “image-name@2x.jpg”.

In case you have a 2 column split screen, we advise to, in case you have 3 tombstones, divide the grid in two vertical cards followed by a third one horizontal. Find more about Cards and Tombstones at the components section of this document.

Iconograpy

Attends’ icons are visual symbols used to represent ideas, objects, or actions. They communicate messages at a glance, afford interactivity, and draw attention to important information. They draw from details found in the Gotham typeface and work well at small sizes.

Usage for designers

The UI icons that appear within Attends components are generally 16px squares, however icons sized at 20px, 24px, 32px and 36px can be also used within the UI. Icons should only be used at their original sizes and not resized in code unless they are exported in .SVG file type, a powerful vector format that allows multiple resizing without losing quality nor resolution.

Touch Targets

All touch targets for interactive icons need to be 42px or larger. Developers can add padding to a touch target with CSS to meet the 42px requirement.

Color

Icons are always a solid, monochromatic or double colored and need to pass the same color contrast ratio as typography. The color of the icon should reflect the importance of the icon’s action which should always be to help guide a user. It’s also important to note that Attends icons themselves do not have interaction states, only their backgrounds do.

Alignment

When used next to text, icons should be center-aligned.

Usage for Developers

Although PNG and JPEG formats can be used, we advise the SVG use approach due to the responsiveness of being a vector file and the capacity of higher performance and less loading time. Also, there are two ways of working with SVGs, either as a single file icon or as a sprite of icons in a file. This last one is the best option when it comes down to maximizing performance. An SVG image sprite is an SVG file containing multiple images (e.g., icons). Unlike SVG symbols (more on this technique later), the images included in an SVG sprite are distributed in a grid.

Combining more images in a single file is a performance booster, as opposed to creating a different file for each image. The main reason being a reduction of the HTTP requests. Reducing HTTP requests, in combo with a caching system for your assets, allows your users to download images in no time!
